02 · KEY FACTS
What makes HLS distinctive
Platform support

HLS is required on Safari and iOS — Apple does not support DASH natively. Any streaming service targeting Apple devices must support HLS. It's also supported on Android, smart TVs, and Roku, making it effectively universal.

Segment containers

Originally HLS used MPEG-2 Transport Stream (.ts) — a 1990s broadcast format. Modern HLS supports fragmented MP4 (.fmp4), which is more efficient and enables full CMAF compatibility with DASH.

Low-Latency HLS (LL-HLS)

Apple introduced LL-HLS in 2019 to bring live latency under 3 seconds. It uses partial segments and preload hints — the player starts downloading a segment before it's fully encoded, staying just seconds behind live.

DRM: FairPlay

Apple's DRM system for HLS is FairPlay Streaming — only decryptable on Apple devices. CMAF's Common Encryption (CENC) allows FairPlay and Widevine to coexist in one encrypted file, solving the dual-DRM problem.
